Per-litre basis so the border gap is price, not basket size – NI from the 900 L survey, ROI from the 1 000 L bulletin, and the two axes locked through the day's ECB rate so vertical position compares directly. Homes on both sides of the border burn the same C2 kerosene; the ROI series arrives under the EU bulletin's "heating gas oil" heading and is treated as an indicator of the ROI heating-oil price level† pending the official kerosene series. Dashed lines strip the tax – ROI from the bulletin's without-taxes series, NI by removing the 5% VAT, kerosene's only NI tax – so the distance between solid and dashed is the tax wedge, live. The grey line is Great Britain, daily – the same tax regime as NI, so the NI–GB gap is pure market structure: half a million oil-heated homes in a compact geography make NI's the densest, most competitive kerosene market in Europe. Vertical markers are policy events. Same fuel, two price regimes, one island.

Cost of one useful kWh of heat by route, at live oil prices and standard tariffs†. The air-source SPF is modelled from each jurisdiction's own climate – HDD-weighted outdoor temperature through a Carnot-fraction COP with defrost and hot-water shares†, calibrated to field-trial medians – so it moves with the weather year, not the brochure. Geothermal's stable source temperature is exactly why its SPF escapes this ceiling. Time-of-use and night tariffs materially improve the heat-pump case; this is the standard-tariff view.

Geothermal – the empty bar

Operational deep geothermal heat on the island of Ireland today is close to zero. The Permo-Triassic Sherwood Sandstone beneath both jurisdictions can supply heat at town scale (Todd et al., Geoenergy, 2026 – 10.1144/geoenergy2025-057). NI's licensing framework is in consultation with a register expected ~2027; this dashboard is built to ingest it the day it exists. Method & sources

Annual buildings-heat anchors – SEAI Energy in Ireland 2025 and National Energy Balance (ROI); DfE Energy in Northern Ireland and NISRA Continuous Household Survey (NI) – are shaped by each week's population-weighted heating degree days (ERA5 via Open-Meteo). Gas gets a live regression where daily data exists; oil, the island's majority fuel, has no daily data anywhere – it is carried as an anchor plus a live price.
