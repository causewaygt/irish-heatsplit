Geothermal – the empty bar

Operational deep geothermal heat on the island of Ireland today is close to zero. The Permo-Triassic Sherwood Sandstone beneath both jurisdictions can supply heat at town scale (Todd et al., Geoenergy, 2026 – 10.1144/geoenergy2025-057). NI's licensing framework is in consultation with a register expected ~2027; this dashboard is built to ingest it the day it exists. The gap between the bar below and the what-if strip above is the argument.

Method & sources

Annual buildings-heat anchors – SEAI Energy in Ireland 2025 and National Energy Balance (ROI); DfE Energy in Northern Ireland and NISRA Continuous Household Survey (NI) – are shaped by each week's population-weighted heating degree days (ERA5 via Open-Meteo). Gas gets a live regression where daily data exists; oil, the island's majority fuel, has no daily data anywhere – it is carried as an anchor plus a live price.
